Welcome. I currently carry out the Wisconsin Idea by assisting communities and organizations in the areas of regional economic analysis, labor force research, socio-economic impact analysis and industry sector competitiveness. My work in GIS and cartography are a large part of this work. My current research emphases include industrial cluster policy, labor mobility, and changes to regional commuting patterns. I was a regular contributor to the Capitol Region Business Journal from 2005 to 2011 and have had my work featured in numerous other business and economic development publications.

What's New?

Stretch Commuting Across the Rural-Urban Continuum

I recently presented new research on patterns and determinants of "stretch" commuters at the 2017 Meetings of the North American Regional Science Council in Vancouver, British Columbia. Stretch commuters are long-distance commuters traveling more than 50 miles each way between their place of residence and place of employment.
