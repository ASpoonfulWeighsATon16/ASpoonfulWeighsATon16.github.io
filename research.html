In addition to my work that is intended for an academic audience, I conduct applied research addressing specific community or regional needs. My areas of emphasis include regional and community economic strategy development, industry cluster analyses, socio-economic impact studies, and policy analysis. I have also given more than 100 presentations, including my "By the Numbers" series that help community leaders, policy makers and other local stakeholders understand how their region is changing and what these changes might mean for the future. Several recent studies and presentations are shared below. Each of these analyses include the use of GIS in a variety of applications. Full reports and presentations can be downloaded by clicking on the respective image.

Recent Applied Research Reports and Presentations

The Buffalo County Economic Recovery strategy was performed under an U.S. Department of Commerce Economic Development Administration grant. The analysis identifies and prioritizes economic development strategies for Buffalo County, Wisconsin, a remote rural county of 13,000 residents that recently experienced the closure of a coal fired power plant

The Abstract of the Madison Region's Agriculture, Food and Beverage Industry Cluster is a comprehensive analysis of these industries in a 14 county region in southcentral and southwest Wisconsin. It was used to help secure an Investing in Manufacturing Communities Partnership (IMCP) designation, only one of 24 in the nation. Led by the U.S. Department of Commerce, IMCP is a multi-agency program designed to leverage economic development funds and accelerate the resurgence of manufacturing.

The Economic Impact of Wisconsin's Military Installations is an analysis of the state's various military installations, including Fort McCoy, the 115th Fighter Wing, the 128th Air Refueling Wing, and the Wisconsin Army National Guard. The study used input-output analysis to estimate direct, indirect and induced impacts for the entire state of Wisconsin and by congressional district.

The Community and Economic Impacts of the St. Croix River Crossing considers how the newly opened St. Croix River Crossing will affect St. Croix County, Wisconsin. The St. Croix River Crossing is a new $700 million bridge connecting western Wisconsin to the eastern portion of the Minneapolis-St. Paul-Bloomington Metropolitan Statistical Area. The analysis includes GIS modeling of joint residence-workplace accessibility both before and after the Crossing's completion.

Windicators is a newsletter published by UW-Extension's Center for Community and Economic Development. It is intended to raise awareness of timely topics affecting Wisconsin's economy and its residents. I authored a recent issue that examined potential labor needs for Foxconn's yet-to-be-built industrial campus in Racine County.

The Wisconsin Economy is a series of reports that include in-depth analyses of various economic topics. However, the reports are written with a general audience in mind, rather than an academic audience. Written in collaboration with colleagues at UW-Madison, the education attainment series examined how educational attainment has changed in Wisconsin.

This presentation was made to the Madison Region's Business Education Collaborative; a group of leaders from educational institutions and private sector industries.

The presentation was presented to stakeholders in Prosperity Southwest Wisconsin, an economic development organization covering five counties in Wisconsin. The group is concerned with labor shortages stemming from an aging and declining population.

This presentation was made at the fall conference of the Wisconsin chapter of the American Planning Association. It considers challenges to talent attraction and retention in Wisconsin given its industrial structure and low gross migration rates over the past few decades.
